Amur Tiger (Siberian Tiger)

Siberian tigers are distinguishable by their striped fur. Similar to people’s unique fingerprints, no two tigers have the same striped pattern.

  • Scientific Name:Panthera tigris altaica
  • Average Weight: 300-500 lb
  • Average Lifespan: 16-18 years
  • Habitat: The Russian Far East and the border areas of China

The Amur tiger, also known as Siberian tiger, is the largest cat in the world. They are solitary animals, marking heir scent on trees to keep other tigers away. They stalk their prey, which include elk, boar, bears, and deer, until they are close enough to pounce. When successful, they drag their kill to a secluded area before devouring the meat.
